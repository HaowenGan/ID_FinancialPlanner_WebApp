What is the 50/30/20 rule?

The 50/30/20 rule is a simple budgeting strategy that allows you to monitor your money effectively, easily, and sustainably. The basic rule of thumb is to divide your monthly after-tax income into three spending categories: 50% for needs(housing, utilities, transportation and other recurring payments), 30% for wants(dining out, clothing, entertainment) and 20% for savings or paying off debt.

You can put your money to work more efficiently if you maintain your costs balanced throughout these major spending categories on a regular basis. As there is only three major categories to track, yuo can save yourself the time and stress of digging into the details every time you spend.
